FIRST Global Mission

To inspire young people to become leaders and innovators in science and technology by engaging them in exciting mentor-based programs that build skills in science, engineering and technology, inspire innovation and foster well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, communication and leadership.

FIRST Kazakhstan

FIRST Kazakhstan is a regional organization that brings the transformative power of FIRST programs to students all across Kazakhstan. We work to expand access to robotics education and competition, giving students the opportunity to develop critical STEM skills.

Our mission is to inspire and prepare the next generation of engineers, scientists and innovators in Kazakhstan through hands-on robotics programs that combine the excitement of sport with the rigor of science and technology.

Through partnerships with schools, universities and industry leaders, FIRST Kazakhstan creates pathways for students to discover their passion for STEM and develop the skills needed to succeed in the 21st century.

Mission

FIRST's mission is to inspire young people to become leaders in science and technology by engaging them in exciting mentor-based programs that build skills in science, engineering and technology. We inspire innovation and foster well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, communication and leadership.

📊

Impact

FIRST programs have reached millions of students around the world. Participants show increased interest in STEM careers, improved problem-solving skills and greater confidence. FIRST alumni are more likely to pursue STEM degrees and careers, contributing to a stronger global innovation economy.

FIRST Programs

🎯

FLL (FIRST LEGO League)

For ages 4-16. Students build robots from LEGO and solve real-world problems through research and innovation projects.

⚙️

FTC (FIRST Tech Challenge)

For grades 7-12. Teams design, build and program robots using reusable platforms to compete in alliance format.

🏆

FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition)

For grades 9-12. The most advanced program, where teams build industrial-sized robots to compete in complex games.

Season Overview

1

Kickoff

The season starts with the game reveal and rules publication. Teams receive their challenge and begin planning.

2

Build Season

Teams design, build and program their robots. Usually 6-8 weeks of intensive development.

3

Competitions

Regional and championship events where teams compete, showcase their work and celebrate achievements.

4

Reflection and Growth

Teams reflect on their season, share learnings and prepare for the next challenge.
